## Approval and correction log

- The work plan says IRI conceptualised **five** programme areas but documents only four. The site publishes only the four documented areas.
- The annual total (UGX 3,061,174,500) and five-year total (UGX 15,305,872,500) are present, but detailed tables require professional verification and are not public.
- “The organization is based in Uganda operates in the whole country” is grammatically incomplete and makes an unverified national operational-reach claim. The public site says only that IRI is Ugandan.
- “Hosting Refugees” appears to be an error; likely intended meaning is host communities. Confirm.
- “Seismic poverty and shortage” is unclear and has not been reproduced.
- “Primary education children” was edited to “children of primary-school age”. Programme eligibility and safeguarding processes need approval.
- Proposed health activities could be read as clinical service provision. Public copy limits IRI’s role to education, outreach, first aid and responsible referral, and does not describe IRI as a licensed facility.
- “Refugees” appears repeatedly where “community” was likely intended, including community workers, development and meetings. Copy has been edited contextually and requires owner review.
- Founding date, leadership names, biographies, physical address, telephone number, social accounts, partners, donors, completed projects and verified impact data are absent.
- The organisation has confirmed the `ineza.org.ug` domain and `info@ineza.org.ug` is now presented without the previous confirmation warning.
- Safeguarding reporting details, privacy retention periods, complaints process, approved policies and audited reports are missing.
- Donation and payment channels are not approved. The site intentionally processes no payments.
- The embedded mark is a 95×95 px brown family silhouette. It is retained for reference but needs a high-resolution or vector master and formal brand approval.
- A curated collection of user-supplied photographs is integrated across the multi-page website as clearly labelled thematic samples. They must not be described as IRI activities or participants. Copyright ownership, licences, model/guardian consent, child-safeguarding review, accurate locations and captions all require confirmation before public deployment. Images depicting acute distress, coercion, illness, medical procedures, watermarks or prominent third-party branding were deliberately excluded.

## Editorial changes

British English, sentence case and refugee-centred terminology are used. Planned activities remain explicitly labelled. No targets have been presented as achievements.
